2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the owners of the site.

Cookies allow a website to recognize your device and remember if you've been to the website before. They help websites understand how you interact with their content, enabling them to improve your browsing experience.

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, OKI AI) are called "first-party cookies." Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called "third-party cookies." Third-party cookies enable certain features or functionalities provided by third parties, such as analytics, marketing, or social media features.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We use different types of cookies for various purposes. Some cookies are essential for the functioning of our Services, while others help us improve performance, provide additional features, or allow us to offer relevant content and advertising.

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the functioning of our Services and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as logging in, setting your privacy preferences, filling in forms, or using secure areas of the website.

You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the Services will not function properly if you do so.

Expiration: Most essential cookies expire when you close your browser (session cookies), while others remain on your device until they expire or you delete them.

Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages. If you disable these cookies, some or all of these features may not function properly.

Functional cookies help us remember your preferences (like language preference or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features.

Expiration: Most functional cookies persist for a few hours to 2 years.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our Services. They help us understand which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors navigate our website.

The information collected through these cookies is aggregated and anonymous. If you disable these cookies, we will not know when you have visited our Services and will not be able to monitor their performance.

Expiration: Most performance and analytics cookies persist for 90 days to 2 years.

Targeting and Marketing Cookies

These cookies may be set through our Services by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant advertisements on other websites.

They do not directly store personal information but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you disable these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising.

Expiration: Most targeting and marketing cookies persist for 90 days to 2 years.

Web3 and DApp Interaction Cookies

As a decentralized AI governance platform, we use specialized cookies to facilitate interactions with blockchain networks and decentralized applications (DApps). These cookies help maintain your connection to blockchain networks, store non-sensitive wallet connection preferences, and improve the performance of decentralized features.

These cookies do not store private keys, seed phrases, or other sensitive wallet information.

Expiration: Most Web3 interaction cookies are session-based and expire when you close your browser, while some configuration preferences may persist for up to 30 days.

5. Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar technologies to store and retrieve information from your devices:

Web Beacons

Web beacons (also known as "pixel tags" or "clear GIFs") are small transparent image files used to monitor your journey around our Services. They can be used together with cookies to track your engagement with our content.

Local Storage

HTML5 local storage allows websites to store information locally on your device. This data persists even after you close your browser and does not expire automatically.

Session Storage

Similar to local storage, but the data is cleared when you close your browser session.

ETags

ETags are identifiers assigned to specific versions of web resources. They can be used for caching and to track user behavior.
